## Mental Snapshot 6: The Formal Paper

### Transition

Notebook done. Now formalize for researchers.

### The Challenge

Make it **rigorous** without losing **accessibility**.

Mathematicians demand proofs. But I want philosophers and physicists to read it too.

### The Solution

Layered formalism:

- **Definitions precise but motivated** (explain WHY we need category theory)
- **Proofs complete but readable** (Shannon's theorem is well-known, cite it)
- **Examples throughout** (Minecraft grounds abstract theory)

### The Structure

```
Abstract ‚Üí Intro ‚Üí Framework ‚Üí Validation ‚Üí Example ‚Üí Implications ‚Üí Open Questions ‚Üí Conclusion
```

Standard academic paper format, but:

- **Abstract** is genuinely accessible
- **Intro** hooks non-experts
- **Example** (Minecraft) is in the BODY, not appendix
- **Open questions** invite collaboration

### What I Learned

**Rigor and accessibility aren't opposed.**

You can write precisely AND clearly. It just takes effort.
