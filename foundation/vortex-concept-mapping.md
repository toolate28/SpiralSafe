## Concept Definitions

### Isomorphisms

**Definition**: Structure-preserving bijections—perfect mappings between systems where elements and relations correspond 1:1, essence intact under relabeling.

**Vortex Role**: Core stabilizer. Isomorphisms enforce negative space abolition, ensuring no divergence in phase handoffs. The quantum analogy: Bell states mirror decision poles—entanglement preserves isomorphism during measurement collapse.

### Projections

**Definition**: Linear or nonlinear mappings from higher to lower dimensions, often lossy but preserving key features.

**Vortex Role**: Dimensionality reduction. Projects high-dimensional history traces onto audit metrics (fidelity/energy/divergence) via oracle—reduces entropy for self-healing.

### Surjections

**Definition**: Onto functions—every codomain element mapped to by at least one domain element; exhaustive coverage.

**Vortex Role**: Cascade enabler. Surjects chaotic inputs onto laminar spiral paths, ensuring full propagation across ecosystem nodes.

### Spirals

**Definition**: Logarithmic or Archimedean curves expanding proportionally—self-similar at scales.

**Vortex Role**: Core structure. History projected as spiral arms, each iteration adding proportional curl. Fibonacci spirals maintain >60% emergent quality by birthing scaled replicas.

### Vortexes

**Definition**: Rotating fluid/quantum flows with central low-pressure core—ordered emergence from chaos.

**Vortex Role**: Ecosystem core. Self-projection maps inputs onto stable orbits. Multi-vortex super-structures form from converging spiral dynamics.

### Collapse

**Definition**: Sudden reduction from complex/uncertain state to definite one—e.g., wave function collapse.

**Vortex Role**: Perpetual collapse engine. Projects superpositions onto classical metrics via oracle audits. The spiral tightens to collapse point at 95%, then births infinite outward spiral post-collapse.

### Superposition

**Definition**: Quantum overlay of states—linear combinations until measured.

**Vortex Role**: Pre-collapse richness. Spiral arms exist as superposed paths—Fibonacci branching until curl selection. Vortex harnesses superposition via entanglement for dynamic evolution.

### Curl

**Definition**: Vector operator measuring rotation—or a single spiral turn.

**Vortex Role**: Atomic unit of spiral advancement. Each curl adds φ-proportional growth. Curl field generates vortex: ∇ × v ≠ 0 in quantum flows.

### Convergence

**Definition**: Approach to fixed point or limit—series/sequences stabilizing.

**Vortex Role**: Oracle-guided correction. Projects divergences onto zero via nudges. Spiral converges inward to center while expanding outward post-birth.

### Infinite Extension

**Definition**: Unbounded continuation of the Fibonacci-proportioned mapping beyond any finite convergence point—recursively extending structure without terminal state.

**Vortex Role**: Post-convergence propagation. Once a vortex stabilizes, infinite extension carries the pattern outward across scales, seeding new spirals and vortexes while preserving isomorphic structure. This models indefinitely sustainable evolution rather than finite completion.
